    Venezuela's typical food


    * The Creole Pavilion (Pabellón criollo), full of flavors, is probably the typical Venezuelan food par excellence. Its ingredients are white rice, grated beef, fried banana slices and black fried beans. Like all other dishes, it has its variants according to the region.


    * The origin of the arepa is ancestral. The natives made arepas of a cooked and ground corn mass. Today they are made with white corn flour, salt, water and oil. With the dough they prepare some breads and then they are given the definitive form. There are arepas of pork bacon, sweet arepitarse, arepas "Peeled" with ashes, roasted arepas.


    * The Hallaca is the clear demonstration of the Venezuelan intersection, Already that their Ingredients have different origins: the Banana leaf, used by Native Americans and the Black African The traditional corn mass of Venezuela; Meat, olives, moose and raisins, NET Spanish influence. Hallaca is traditionally served for Christmas..


    * The Sancocho is a thick soup based on cassava, potatoes, Banana and various legumes that are usually added of chicken or beef.

         Other typical Venezuelan foods are: The black roast, the Pasticho, the Cazabe, and the patacones.


         In addition to typical Venezuelan foods, almost all desserts, sweets and treats have indigenous origins. There are abundant ingredients such as plantain, pineapple, guava, orange, coconut, plunes, chocolate, almonds. They are mixed with cinnamon, honey, orange water, syrup, clove, rum or typical paper (sugar cane molasses) to give as a result delicious and unforgettable flavors.


    * Among the desserts of Aboriginal origin is the Majarete, a delight made of coconut milk, flour, cinnamon, paper and sugar.


    * From the time of the colony comes the Bejarana cake, based on banana, mixed with butter, cinnamon powder, paper, white cheese, carnation and syrup.


    * One of the typical treats are the Negritos, whose basis is the chocolate semidoce, cut into pieces, and mixed with flour, butter, nuts and eggs.


    * Among the traditional beverages is chicha, based on corn. Chicha Andina was the ritual drink of the natives of the pre-Columbian era. In our day it is the typical drink of the state of Táchira.


    * Chalky (Tizana), typically Venezuelan, is a refreshing drink without alcohol. It is based on several fruits: pineapple, mango, strawberries, grapes, oranges, apples, papaya, cambure (small banana), bathed with little concentrated lemonade, grenadine and ice.
