    How did Venezuelan culture emerge?


         In Venezuela there are different cultural manifestations, these may vary from region to region. Each cultural manifestation has its own characteristics that make it unique and particular. Venezuela is a very diverse country, whose culture is marked by the indigenous, Spanish and African traditions that converged in the territory during the colonial period. Its inhabitants are also a mixture of races in which it is difficult to find someone who has no indigenous, European or African ancestry. In fact, a lot of people share more than one.

         Currently, there are still indigenous communities that have had little contact with Western civilization, which contrasts much with the large cities full of skyscrapers and roads. These have even some American influence because, despite the marked anticapitalist tone of the current government, Venezuela experienced a strong American influence during the second half of the twentieth century. The discovery of numerous oilfields led the country to American citizens whose fashions and styles were incorporated into the local culture.


         Venezuelans are also very friendly and welcoming people. They like to enjoy their life and the moment, hence their cheerful and festive character marked. If you are in Venezuela, make sure that you will see many parties and you will also be invited to many of them.


         Baseball is the most popular sport in Venezuela, and the Venezuelan Professional Baseball league has existed since 1945. Besides baseball, other popular sports in the country are basketball and football. Venezuela hosted the 2012 pre-Olympic Basketball World Cup and the Copa América de Basquete Masculinode 2013, which took place in the polyhedron of Caracas. Also, hosted the Copa Améica of football at 2007. Football, led by the Venezuelan national football team, is gaining popularity as well.


         Venezuela is also home to Formula 1 rider, Pastor Maldonado. In the Spanish Grand Prix of 2012, he won his first victory and became the first and only Venezuelan to have done this in the entire history of Formula 1. Maldonado has increased the popularity of Formula 1 in Venezuela and is now inspiring thousands of young Venezuelan children to pursue a career in the sport.


         At the 2012 Summer Olympics, Venezuelan Rubén Limardo Won the gold in fencing.

         The Venezuelan music is characterized by the mixture of Spanish, indigenous and African elements, the most representative genus of the country is the joropo, which uses instruments such as harp, Cubatro and maracas. Because of the cultural influence of other countries is heard, hip-hop, dance, techno, rock and pop. The musical taste of the Venezuelan is very different from that of the South American countries, only with Colombia has some common tastes like Ranger joropo because in the eastern plains of Colombia This musical manifestation is produced, being frontier. The musical taste of the country is purely Caribbean, in Venezuela salsa, merengue and Colombian coubia is music to listen to and not just to dance, as in other countries. It is noteworthy that the national music is called Llanera Music, and comes from the main plains of the country (Apure and Barinas States). Due to the Afro-Antillean influence, the CALIPSO and drum are important musical genres in Venezuela. Some of the Venezuelan musical figures include the 19th-century pianist Teresa Carreño, and the great development that happened with the movement of the symphony orchestras of youth.
